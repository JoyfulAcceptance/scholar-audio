# Scholar Audio: 30-Second Demo Capture Plan

## Concept

The demonstration narrates itself. Scholar Audio generates the spoken transcript, and each sentence describes the action visible onscreen at that moment.

The video proves one complete transformation:

```text
Dense scholarly paper → Scholar Audio → chaptered audiobook
```

Target length: **28–30 seconds**, followed by a silent two-second end card if the final export remains comfortably under the intended length.

## Narration transcript

At a baseline of 130 words per minute, this should run close to thirty seconds with Scholar Audio's natural pauses:

> Scholarly papers can be difficult to follow when your eyes need to rest. Scholar Audio prepares them for listening, locally on your Mac. Drop in a paper, choose an installed voice and pace, and create a chaptered audiobook. Download the audio alone, or a listening packet with the original PDF. Keep the paper open for charts and equations while the argument continues in your ears.

Generate this narration through Scholar Audio so the soundtrack is itself a product artifact. Import the resulting audio file directly into the video timeline rather than recording it through the Mac speakers.

## Shot plan

| Time | Narration | Screen action |
|---|---|---|
| 0:00–0:05 | “Scholarly papers can be difficult to follow when your eyes need to rest.” | Show a dense PDF. Move past a difficult paragraph and briefly linger on a chart, table, equation, or image. |

| 0:10–0:16 | “Drop in a paper, choose an installed voice and pace, and create a chaptered audiobook.” | Drop in the paper, show the selected voice and 130 WPM, then click **Create audio**. Show genuine progress briefly and jump cut to completion. |

## Capture method

1. Turn on Do Not Disturb and close unrelated windows and duplicate Scholar Audio tabs.
2. Arrange the browser in a clean 16:9 frame with readable text.
3. Press **Shift–Command–5** and choose **Record Selected Window** or **Record Selected Portion**.
4. Record the five screen actions as separate short clips; do not attempt one continuous take.
5. Capture screen video without room-microphone audio.
6. Assemble the clips in iMovie.
7. Drag the exact Scholar Audio narration file beneath the clips in the timeline.
8. Cut screen actions to the spoken sentence boundaries.
9. Use direct cuts and one honest jump cut during generation; avoid decorative transitions.
10. Export, watch once without stopping, and confirm that every label remains readable on a normal YouTube player.

## Capture preparation

- Use one clean demo PDF that can legally appear in the public video.
- Begin with Scholar Audio freshly loaded and the desired voice already installed.
- Use 130 WPM unless listening tests show a small adjustment is necessary for the final transcript.
- Prepare a clean output location so the result shown belongs unmistakably to this run.
- Confirm both downloads before recording.
- Confirm the generated `.m4b` contains the full source and named chapters.
- Keep the PDF ready at a useful chart, table, equation, or image for the final shot.

## What stays outside the video

The README and Devpost description will carry details that do not fit the thirty-second proof:

- Setup instructions
- Supported input formats
- Pacing-system explanation
- Citation naming and packet contents
- Limitations such as scanned-PDF OCR
- Detailed explanation of how Codex and GPT-5.6 were used
- Required `/feedback` session ID

The video's only job is to make the working product immediately understandable.
