Listening With Tired Eyes
Genevieve Prentice
July 2026

ABSTRACT

Dense papers often arrive when a reader has the least visual energy left. This short sample demonstrates how Scholar Audio turns structured text into named listening tracks while keeping the source document local.

INTRODUCTION

Listening is not identical to reading a page. A useful listening copy should announce changes in structure and give the reader enough space to notice a new idea.

METHODS

Scholar Audio preserves common section headings, repairs simple line-break artifacts, and sends each prepared section to a voice already installed on the reader's Mac.

CONCLUSION

The smallest useful result is a folder that a tired reader can understand: named text sections, matching audio tracks, and a short guide to the packet.
